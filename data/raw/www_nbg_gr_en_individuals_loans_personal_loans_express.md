## Do you want more information?
How can I repay my monthly loan repayments?
You can pay by monthly amortization installments, starting one month after the loan disbursement and payable via the borrower's designated loan-linked Deposit Account with NBG.
How does the Bank notify me about my installments, loan rate, and other details?
A statement is sent to you every month to keep you duly updated on your loan’s remaining instalments, interest rate, outstanding principal etc. Moreover, if you're a Digital Banking user, you can sign up for the Statements service and receive alerts regarding the issuance of your loan statements at the email address you've registered with the Bank.
What is the interest rate of the loan?
The loan is available at a floating rate, based on the 3Μ Euribor, subject to a minimum equal to the ECB's intervention rate, plus a fixed margin rate throughout the entire loan term. Please note that the floating rate fluctuates in line with any increase or decrease in the base rate, which will impact your monthly installments accordingly. The floating rate also includes the levy required under Law 128/75, currently 0.60%.   
  
For further details about the loan rate and expenses, click [here](https://www.nbg.gr/-/jssmedia/Files/Timologio/web_portal_english_epitokia-timologio_daneiwn.pdf).
Can I make an early repayment?
Yes, you can make an early or partial repayment at any time without any additional charge.
How much money can I borrow?
The loan amount can range between €300 and €6,000.
What is the repayment term?
If you borrow less than €1,000, the repayment term is from 12 to 24 months. If you borrow more than €1,000 and up to €3,000, the repayment term is from 12 to 48 months. If you borrow more than €3,000, the repayment term is from 12 to 60 months.
Do I earn Go For More reward points with this loan?
If you have an NBG debit or credit card and are enrolled in the **Go For More** reward program, you earn **Go For More** points every time you pay your instalment on time according to the Terms & Conditions posted on the Bank’s website.
What is the Total Annual Effective Rate (TAER - ΣΕΠΠΕ)?
From 12 to 60 months. The loan term depends on the amount borrowed.  
TARC example: The TARC for a €6,000 loan at 11.80% floating interest rate, plus the 0.60% levy under Law 128/75, for a term of 60 months and one-off fee of €75, amounts to 13.76%*.  
The overall cost of the loan in the above example is €8,155.96.  
*The TARC rate is used merely as an example and applies only if the above assumptions are met.
You can read more about the Total Annual Effective Rate [here](https://www.nbg.gr/-/jssmedia/Files/Idiwtes/Forms-for-loans/SEPPE-CONSUMER-LOANS-ENG.pdf).
What happens if I don't get the loan statement on time?
Every month we'll send you the notification to the address you've given us. If the statement is not delivered to you for any reason beyond the Bank's control, the installment will nevertheless still be due for collection, as required under the loan agreement. For further information, please visit your NBG Branch.
What happens if I fail to pay an installment by the due date?
You should settle your loan payments as soon as possible. An unpaid instalment incurs default interest and affects your credit profile.
How can I change my personal details?
To change or update your details you’ll need your NBG Digital Banking codes, and your taxisnet codes in order to login to eGov-KYC. Through the eGov-KYC service, you can change your income, contact, address and occupation details or upload the corresponding supporting documents.
